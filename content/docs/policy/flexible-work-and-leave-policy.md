# Flexible Work and Leave Policy

**Document Version:** 3.2  
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** February 15, 2024  
**Review Date:** March 2025  
**Owner:** Mei Lin, Director of Finance & Operations  
**Approved By:** Catherine Wu, Managing Partner  

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy outlines Meridian Advisory's approach to flexible work arrangements, leave entitlements, and work-life balance considerations. Given the demanding nature of management consulting and our commitment to maintaining exceptional client service standards, this policy seeks to balance operational requirements with employee wellbeing and retention.

This policy applies to all permanent employees at Meridian Advisory. Contractors and temporary staff are covered under separate arrangements outlined in their individual agreements.

## 2. Flexible Work Arrangements

### 2.1 Remote Work

**Eligibility:** All employees who have completed their probationary period (6 months) and maintain satisfactory performance ratings.

**Standard Arrangements:**
- Up to 2 days per week remote work for consultants and senior staff
- Up to 3 days per week for Director level and above
- Partners maintain discretion over their arrangements based on client commitments

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 3 days per week in Perth office for face-to-face collaboration
- Client site attendance as required (typically 2-3 days per week during active engagements)
- Participation in all scheduled team meetings and client presentations
- Maintenance of professional home office setup with reliable internet (minimum 50 Mbps)

**Approval Process:** Submit Form HR-07 to direct manager with 2 weeks notice. Director level approval required for arrangements exceeding standard allocations.

*Note: Remote work policies were accelerated during COVID-19 and remain under review. The current 60/40 office/remote split may be adjusted based on the outcome of our Q2 2024 space utilization study.*

### 2.2 Flexible Hours

**Core Business Hours:** 9:00 AM - 4:00 PM AWST (all staff must be available)

**Flexible Start/End Times:**
- Start time: 7:00 AM - 10:00 AM
- End time: 4:00 PM - 7:00 PM (minimum 8-hour day)

**Client Considerations:** Eastern states client meetings may require early starts (7:00 AM for Sydney/Melbourne calls). International work may require evening availability.

**Compressed Work Weeks:** Available on a case-by-case basis, typically 4x10 hour days. Requires partner approval and cannot coincide with major client deliverables.

## 3. Annual Leave

### 3.1 Standard Entitlements

- **20 days** annual leave per year (pro-rated for part-time staff)
- **10 days** personal/sick leave per year
- **2 days** compassionate leave as needed
- **Long Service Leave** as per WA legislation (8.67 weeks after 10 years)

### 3.2 Additional Leave Provisions

**Professional Development Leave:** Up to 3 days annually for relevant conferences, certifications, or training. Must be pre-approved and align with firm capability development goals.

**Study Leave:** Employees pursuing relevant qualifications (MBA, industry certifications) may access up to 5 days annually. Requires signed development agreement.

**Sabbatical Leave:** After 5 years of service, employees may apply for unpaid sabbatical leave (1-6 months). Subject to business requirements and succession planning.

### 3.3 Leave Scheduling

**Blackout Periods:**
- December 1-31 (limited approval, maximum 2 people per team)
- Major client deliverable weeks (as designated by project partners)
- Month-end financial reporting periods (finance team only)

**Advance Notice:**
- 4 weeks minimum for leave exceeding 1 week
- 2 weeks minimum for single week periods
- 48 hours for emergency personal leave

**Coverage Requirements:** All leave must include documented handover of client responsibilities and internal tasks. Use Template DOC-14 for handover documentation.

## 4. Purchased Annual Leave

### 4.1 Scheme Overview

Employees may purchase up to 10 additional days of annual leave per year through salary sacrifice arrangements. Popular option given consulting industry demands and client travel requirements.

**Cost Calculation:** Annual salary ÷ 252 working days × number of purchased days

**Payment Deduction:** Spread evenly across 26 pay periods from July 1

**Eligibility:** All permanent employees after 12 months of service

### 4.2 Application Process

Applications open April 1-30 each year for the following financial year. Submit Form HR-12 to Mei Lin with manager endorsement.

*Current uptake: 67% of eligible employees (30 of 45) participate in purchased leave scheme.*

## 5. Special Leave Considerations

### 5.1 Client Travel Impact

Extended client site assignments may qualify for additional leave considerations:

- **Interstate assignments >2 weeks:** 1 additional day per month away
- **International travel:** 1 day per 7 days traveled (cumulative quarterly)
- **Mining site rotations:** Special FIFO arrangements as negotiated

### 5.2 Government Engagement Leave

Staff working on government contracts may be eligible for additional public service holidays where client site requires attendance on WA public holidays not observed by client agencies.

### 5.3 Partner Leave Arrangements

Partners maintain different leave structures reflecting ownership stake and client responsibility. Details covered in separate Partner Agreement documentation.

## 6. Performance and Accountability

### 6.1 Flexible Work Performance Standards

All flexible work arrangements are subject to:
- Maintenance of billable hour targets (currently 1,680 hours annually for consultants)
- Client satisfaction scores of 4.0/5.0 or higher
- Timely completion of internal deliverables and administrative requirements

### 6.2 Review Process

**Quarterly Reviews:** Manager assessment of flexible work arrangement effectiveness
**Annual Review:** Comprehensive evaluation including client feedback and peer input
**Immediate Review:** Triggered by performance concerns or client complaints

Failure to meet standards may result in modification or termination of flexible arrangements.

## 7. Technology and Security Requirements

### 7.1 Remote Work Technology

**Required Equipment:**
- Company laptop with VPN access
- Dual monitor setup for home office
- Headset suitable for client calls
- Secure file storage (no client data on personal devices)

**Security Protocols:**
- Multi-factor authentication for all systems
- Encrypted communications for client discussions
- Annual cybersecurity training (managed by Daniel Okafor's team)

### 7.2 Client Confidentiality

Enhanced confidentiality protocols apply to remote work given the sensitive nature of client engagements. Refer to Information Security Policy IS-001 for detailed requirements.

## 8. Implementation and Exceptions

### 8.1 Transition Period

Current flexible arrangements established during 2020-2022 are grandfathered until March 2025. All arrangements will be reviewed against these updated standards during annual performance cycles.

### 8.2 Exception Process

Requests for arrangements outside policy parameters require:
1. Written business case submission
2. Client impact assessment
3. Partner panel approval (Catherine Wu, Daniel Okafor, Raj Kapoor)

### 8.3 Emergency Procedures

During client emergencies or critical project phases, all flexible arrangements may be temporarily suspended with 24-hour notice. Emergency response procedures detailed in Operations Manual OP-003.
