# Conflict of Interest and Independence Policy

**Document Version:** 3.2  
**Effective Date:** 1 March 2023  
**Last Review:** 15 February 2024  
**Next Review:** March 2025  
**Owner:** Catherine Wu, Managing Partner  
**Approved by:** Partnership Committee  

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes the framework for identifying, assessing, and managing conflicts of interest to maintain Meridian Advisory's independence and professional integrity. All personnel must comply with this policy when engaging with clients, potential clients, suppliers, competitors, or other stakeholders.

This policy applies to all Meridian Advisory employees, partners, contractors, and associates ("Personnel"). Special provisions apply to government engagements given our significant exposure to state and federal departments across Western Australia.

## 2. Definitions

### 2.1 Conflict of Interest
Any situation where personal interests, relationships, or obligations could compromise or appear to compromise professional judgment in client service delivery.

### 2.2 Independence Threat
Circumstances that could impair objectivity or create perception of bias, including:
- Financial interests in client organizations
- Personal relationships with client personnel
- Previous employment with clients
- Concurrent services creating self-review threats
- Advocacy or management functions for clients

### 2.3 Government Client
Any Commonwealth, state, or local government department, agency, government trading enterprise, or statutory authority.

## 3. Conflict Identification and Assessment

### 3.1 Mandatory Disclosure Requirements

All Personnel must disclose potential conflicts through the following mechanisms:

**Annual Declarations (due 30 June)**
- Direct financial interests in current or potential clients
- Immediate family employment with clients or competitors
- Outside directorships or advisory roles
- Significant personal relationships with client personnel
- Previous employment history (last 5 years)

**Engagement-Specific Declarations**
- Must be completed before commencing any new client work
- Required for scope extensions or changes in engagement teams
- Immediate disclosure of conflicts arising during engagements

**Ad-Hoc Reporting**
- Material changes in personal circumstances
- New relationships or financial interests
- Approaches for employment from clients

### 3.2 Assessment Criteria

The Partnership Committee evaluates conflicts using a three-tier framework:

**Category 1 - Prohibited**
- Direct financial interest >1% in publicly listed clients
- Immediate family in senior management roles (Director level+)
- Current employment discussions with client organizations
- Provision of bookkeeping or accounting services to audit clients

**Category 2 - Manageable with Safeguards**
- Minor financial interests (<$10,000 or <1% ownership)
- Extended family relationships (cousins, in-laws)
- Previous employment >3 years ago
- Non-competing concurrent client relationships

**Category 3 - Monitor and Review**
- Social relationships with client personnel
- Alumni networks or professional associations
- Industry forum participation
- Prior consulting relationships >5 years ago

## 4. Government Engagement Requirements

### 4.1 Enhanced Independence Standards

Government work requires heightened independence due to public interest considerations:

**Pre-Engagement Screening**
- All engagement team members complete Government Conflict Declaration (Form CI-Gov)
- Enhanced background checks for sensitive engagements
- Verification of political donations and affiliations
- Assessment of family connections to political parties or officials

**Ongoing Monitoring**
- Quarterly reviews during extended government engagements
- Immediate reporting of political activities or donations
- Annual confirmation of continued independence

### 4.2 Political Activities Restrictions

Personnel on government engagements must:
- Avoid political party membership or office-holding
- Refrain from public political advocacy or campaigning
- Disclose political donations >$1,000 annually
- Seek approval for participation in policy forums or submissions

*Note: These restrictions apply for the duration of government engagements plus 6 months post-completion.*

## 5. Cooling-Off Periods

### 5.1 Standard Cooling-Off Requirements

**Client to Meridian Employment**
- Senior client personnel (Director+): 12 months before joining Meridian
- Other client personnel: 6 months before involvement in related engagements

**Meridian to Client Employment**
- Partners/Directors: 18 months restriction on client-related services
- Senior Consultants: 12 months restriction on direct client contact
- Other personnel: 6 months restriction on related engagements

### 5.2 Government-Specific Cooling-Off

Extended cooling-off periods apply for government transitions:

**Government to Meridian**
- Ministers/Parliamentary Secretaries: 24 months
- Senior Executive Service (SES Band 2+): 18 months
- Other SES officers: 12 months
- Executive Level officers: 6 months

**Meridian to Government**
- Partners involved in government work: 18 months before government employment
- Applies to Commonwealth, state, and local government positions
- Includes statutory authorities and government trading enterprises

## 6. Management Strategies

### 6.1 Structural Safeguards

**Information Barriers**
- Physical separation of conflicted personnel from engagement teams
- Restricted access to confidential client information systems
- Separate reporting lines for conflicted engagements

**Independent Review**
- External quality assurance for high-risk engagements
- Peer review by non-conflicted partners
- Client confirmation of independence arrangements

### 6.2 Disclosure and Consent

When conflicts are manageable through safeguards:
- Written disclosure to affected clients within 5 business days
- Detailed explanation of conflict nature and proposed management
- Documented client consent before proceeding
- Regular confirmation of continued acceptance

## 7. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited regardless of safeguards:

**Financial Relationships**
- Direct investment in clients during engagements
- Loans or guarantees involving client personnel
- Joint business ventures with current clients
- Commission or finder's fee arrangements

**Inappropriate Relationships**
- Romantic relationships between engagement team and client personnel
- Gift acceptance beyond nominal value ($100 limit)
- Entertainment beyond reasonable business purposes
- Personal favors or services for client personnel

## 8. Monitoring and Compliance

### 8.1 Ongoing Monitoring System

**Quarterly Independence Confirmations**
- All engagement teams complete independence checklists
- Random sampling of declarations for verification
- Review of expense reports for inappropriate client entertainment
- Cross-referencing with public company announcements and press

**Annual Compliance Review**
- Partnership Committee reviews all conflict disclosures
- Assessment of safeguard effectiveness
- Client feedback on independence perceptions
- Update of conflict database and registers

### 8.2 Exception Reporting

The Partnership Committee maintains registers of:
- All identified conflicts and their resolution
- Cooling-off period exemptions and justifications
- Client complaints or concerns regarding independence
- Regulatory inquiries or professional body investigations

*Note: The current exception register includes ongoing monitoring of Raj Kapoor's previous Department of Treasury employment (cooling-off period expires September 2024) and Daniel Okafor's spouse's employment with Rio Tinto Limited (managed through information barriers).*

## 9. Training and Communication

### 9.1 Mandatory Training

All Personnel must complete:
- Annual conflict of interest training (due by 30 July)
- Government-specific independence training for relevant personnel
- New joiner orientation including conflict identification
- Refresher training following policy updates

### 9.2 Resources and Support

Personnel can access guidance through:
- Independence helpline (managed by Mei Lin, Director of Finance & Operations)
- Quarterly "Independence Insights" briefings
- Template disclosure letters and client communications
- External legal counsel for complex situations

## 10. Enforcement and Consequences

### 10.1 Policy Violations

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:
- Formal counseling and additional training
- Removal from specific client engagements
- Financial penalties (partner profit share adjustments)
- Termination of employment for serious breaches

### 10.2 Reporting Violations

Personnel are required to report suspected policy violations to:
- Direct supervisors or engagement partners
- Mei Lin (confidential reporting line)
- Catherine Wu for partner-level concerns
- External whistleblower hotline for serious misconduct

## 11. Review and Updates

This policy is reviewed annually by the Partnership Committee with input from:
- External legal counsel specializing in professional services regulation
- Professional body guidance updates (CPA Australia, ICAA)
- Government procurement policy changes
- Industry best practice developments

Recent updates (February 2024) included enhanced government cooling-off periods and clarification of political activities restrictions following new Commonwealth procurement guidelines.
